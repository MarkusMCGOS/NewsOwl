OPINION: This is why Trump won the 2024 U.S. Election

By Adam Shen
Contributions and Edits by Markus Lau
12/05/2024

The U.S. election has recently concluded, and Donald Trump has won the 2024 U.S. election. He is now the 47th president of the United States, contrary to news poll predictions. But how did this happen?

  1. America's readiness for a woman to be president

    According to a poll by the Pew Research Center, it showed that around 47% of women feel that a woman president would be better at “working out compromises”, which is more than the 30% of men. This shows that a percent of America is still uncertain about the fact of a woman being president.

    Armed with this fact, we can safely assume that some people chose Donald Trump not because of liking his policies or personality, but because they aren't ready for a woman to be president.

  2. Harris's connection to Biden's campaign

    President Biden faced lots of criticism during his time in office. Many people were concerned about the inflation that grew while he was in office, or the policies around immigration and the border, among other things.

    When Kamala's campaign first started, she went to the ABC's “The View” to get voters to know her better. However, when she was asked how she would space herself from the Biden campaign, she said: “Not a thing that comes to mind”. That was a fatal flaw, as Biden only had an average of 40% approval rating.

    In the end, she wasn't able to space herself far away enough from the Biden campaign, causing her to probably lose many millions of votes.

  3. Trump's campaign strategy

    In many of Trump's speeches, he has claimed that America is on a downward trend and will fall to the hands of the enemies. By saying things like this, he is driving fear into his supporters and other voters.

    If someone says that the world is about to get destroyed by an asteroid, and claims that they have a way to prevent that from happening, I probably wouldn't believe them. But if they repeat it many times, and there are many people around me believing them, I might also slowly start to get convinced. That could possibly be one of Trump's strategies; to make himself look like the hero that can save the country, encouraging people to vote for him.

  4. Following through with promises

    On the day before the election, November 4, a national NPR/PBS/Marist poll concluded that Kamala Harris holds a slight edge over Donald Trump among likely voters. However, many undecided voters aren't convinced whether or not Harris will actually follow through with her policies.

    Due to this, many people either didn't vote, or voted for Trump because they didn't get to know her well enough.

  5. The economic state

    For many years, some people have been annoyed at the poor economy. Things like inflation, wages, and employment have put many people in a sour mood. According to an ABC/Ipsos poll, approximately 76% of Americans believe that the economy is going in the wrong direction. Also, considering that the Republican Party has very strong policies about fixing the economy, many people would already be leaning towards Trump.

    Connecting back to the beginning, it was during the Biden Administration when things went south, so Biden's approval rate is only at 40%. In addition, Harris wasn't able to separate herself away from Biden well enough, as stated in point 2.

There are several main reasons on how Trump won the 2024 US Election, contrary to news poll predictions. Sometimes, things won't always go the way they seem to be heading, such as in 2016, with Hillary Clinton. Thanks for reading this NewsOwl Opinion article.
